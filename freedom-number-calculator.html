What Is a Financial Freedom Number?

Your financial freedom number (also called your "Safe Number" or "freedom number") is the monthly income amount that covers all your essential expenses and gives you peace of mind. It's the number that, once reached, means you're no longer financially stressed.

Unlike the concept of "financial independence" (which typically requires enough assets to never work again), your freedom number is a more achievable, near-term goal. It's the income level that unlocks options — the ability to be selective about your work, take risks, and pursue what matters most.

How to Calculate Your Freedom Number

Your freedom number is calculated by adding up all your essential monthly expenses:

  • Housing (rent or mortgage)
  • Food and groceries
  • Transportation
  • Utilities and bills
  • Health and insurance
  • Any other essential expenses

Add your monthly savings goal to this total, and you have your Freedom Number. The calculator above does this automatically.

Why Your Freedom Number Matters

Most people have a vague sense that they want to "make more money" or "be financially secure." But without a specific number, this goal is impossible to track or achieve.

Your Freedom Number transforms a vague aspiration into a concrete, trackable goal. When you know your number, you can:

  • Make better decisions about your career and income streams
  • Track your progress with clarity and motivation
  • Know exactly when you've achieved financial peace of mind
  • Set meaningful milestones on the path to your goal
